The Challenge

Why Photo Selection Hurts

Culling is the most tedious bottleneck in a professional photographer's workflow.

⏱️

Hours of Manual Sorting

Wedding and event shoots result in thousands of RAW images. Sifting through every photo to select keepers is an exhausting chore.

🧠

Decision Fatigue

Constantly comparing almost-identical photos to select the single best frame drains your energy, slowing down the editing stage.

👁️

Missed Flaws

It's easy to miss micro-imperfections like closed eyes, slight motion blur, or sub-optimal facial expressions until the final export.

⚙️

Workflow Disconnect

Importing, flagging, and moving files across different editing suites like Capture One and Lightroom causes unnecessary friction.

Our Approach

Privacy-First Intelligent Culling

We leverage Apple Silicon and native frameworks to analyze your photo library locally with zero cloud dependencies.

1

Near-Duplicate Clustering

Groups photos into visual "stacks" using cosine similarity on high-dimensional feature print embeddings, keeping your workspace clean and categorized.

2

Technical Defect Analysis

Runs local Laplacian variance edge detection for tack-sharp focus evaluation, alongside blink detection (Eye Aspect Ratio) and aesthetic grading.

3

Automatic 5-Star Rating

Grades photo quality into a standardized 5-star scale, automatically promoting the highest quality "stack leaders" while downgrading redundant duplicates.

Core Technical Stack macOS Native

  • Apple Vision Framework: Extracting feature print embeddings and detecting eye / face landmarks entirely offline.
  • Accelerate Framework: vImage & vDSP DSP vector math library for hardware-accelerated Laplacian calculations.
  • Metadata & XMP Sidecars: Fully compatible with Lightroom and Capture One. Reads/writes standard ratings with no proprietary locks.
  • Capture One Integration: Direct script triggers and physical selects sorting to speed up active editing sessions.
Rating Tuning

Customizing Quality Weights

Fine-tune the scoring engine to prioritize composition, sharpness, or facial expressions depending on the shoot.

Culler.ai features a flexible Weighted Rating Mode that evaluates every photo across four quality metrics, merging them into a composite quality score (0.0 to 1.0) before applying star ratings.

  • Sharpness (20% default): Uses hardware-accelerated Laplacian variance to evaluate focus. Critical for action or sports shoots.
  • Composition & Aesthetics (35% default): Vision model scores framing, contrast, lighting, and general visual appeal. Ideal for landscapes and scenery.
  • Face Quality (30% default): Evaluates face posture, visibility, and details. Essential for portraits and headshots.
  • Eye Aspect Ratio / EAR (15% default): Detects closed eyes or blinks to lower the score of ruined candid portraits.

Proportional Normalization: Your slider settings are raw weights. If their sum is not 100%, the engine automatically normalizes them proportionally so they sum to 100% internally. Use the interactive panel to experiment with how weights normalize!

⚠️

macOS Gatekeeper Warning

When you double-click the app for the first time, macOS may say "App is damaged and can't be opened" or "Developer cannot be verified" because of ad-hoc signature parameters.

You can easily skip this security quarantine block using one of the three following methods to register a system-wide developer exemption:

🖱️ Option 1: Right-Click Bypass (Recommended)

Control-click (or right-click) the Culler.ai.app bundle in Finder, select Open, and click the confirmation dialog's Open button. macOS saves this choice automatically.

⚙️ Option 2: System Settings Option

Open the app once. When the popup blocks you, go to macOS System Settings > Privacy & Security, scroll down to the Security category, and click Open Anyway.

💻 Option 3: Command-Line Bypassing

If you prefer using the terminal, clear the system quarantine metadata flag directly (assuming the app is in your `/Applications` directory):

xattr -r -d com.apple.quarantine /Applications/Culler.ai.app
